Research Summary

Hamza's research centers on how deformable bio-inspired bodies interact with flow and how those interactions can be utilized for practical engineering functions. His recent work spans flow-energy harvesting, hydrodynamic sensing, EMG-informed fish kinematics, and interpretable model transfer from biology to robotics.

He also has a background in aerodynamic design, including wing shape optimization, vortex generators, and UAV design.

Publications

A Geometric Optimization Framework for a Bio-inspired Flexible Hydrofoil Energy Harvester
Article, March 2026

Develops a koi-carp-derived flexible hydrofoil and a surrogate-based geometric optimization framework for flow-energy harvesting, showing strong performance gains over conventional benchmark foils.

Bio-to-Robot Transfer of Fish Sensorimotor Dynamics via Interpretable Model
Article, December 2025

Presents an interpretable system-identification framework that maps between EMG signals and fish kinematics, with implications for transferring biological sensorimotor principles to robotics.

Beyond Propulsion: Muscle Proprioception Enables Hydrodynamic Sensing in Fish Body
Article, October 2025

Investigates synchronized multi-channel EMG and body kinematics in freely swimming fish to study how muscle activity may contribute to hydrodynamic sensing in complex flows.
